# Transportation Guide: Trains and Buses in Japan

## Overview

Japan has one of the world's most efficient, punctual, and extensive public transportation systems. Understanding the basics will make your trip much smoother.

## Train Systems

### JR (Japan Railways)

**What it is:** The main railway company operating nationwide trains, including the famous Shinkansen (bullet trains).

**Key JR Lines in Tokyo:**

- **JR Yamanote Line** - Circular loop connecting major stations (Shibuya, Shinjuku, Tokyo Station, Ueno, Ikebukuro)
- **JR Chuo Line** - East-west line through central Tokyo
- **JR Sobu Line** - Connects Tokyo Station to eastern areas
- **JR Keihin-Tohoku Line** - North-south line parallel to Yamanote

**Key JR Lines in Kansai (Kyoto/Osaka):**

- **JR Tokaido Line** - Connects Kyoto, Osaka, and Kobe
- **JR Osaka Loop Line** - Circular line around Osaka
- **JR Nara Line** - Kyoto to Nara (for Fushimi Inari and Nara deer park)
- **JR Sagano Line** - Kyoto to Arashiyama

**Shinkansen (Bullet Train):**

- **Tokaido Shinkansen** - Tokyo ↔ Kyoto ↔ Osaka (2.5-3 hours Tokyo to Kyoto)
- **Train types:**
  - **Nozomi** - Fastest (2h 15min Tokyo-Kyoto), NOT covered by JR Pass
    - Stops only at major stations (Tokyo, Shinagawa, Nagoya, Kyoto, Osaka)
    - Most frequent service (3-4 trains per hour)
    - Premium pricing
  - **Hikari** - Fast (2h 45min Tokyo-Kyoto), covered by JR Pass, good choice
    - Stops at more stations than Nozomi
    - 1-2 trains per hour
    - Best value for JR Pass holders
  - **Kodama** - All stations (3h 45min Tokyo-Kyoto), covered by JR Pass
    - Stops at every station along the route
    - Least frequent service
    - Good for shorter distances
- **Reserved vs Non-Reserved:**
  - Reserved seats (shitei-seki) recommended for families with luggage
  - Non-reserved cars (jiyū-seki) available but can be crowded
  - Green Car (first class) available for extra comfort
  - Reserved seat fee: ¥530-1,340 depending on season and distance
- **Luggage:**
  - Overhead racks fit bags up to 40cm x 25cm x 55cm
  - Space behind last row seats for large luggage (160-250cm total dimensions)
  - **New rule (2020+):** Oversized luggage (160-250cm) requires reservation
  - Luggage forwarding (takkyubin) recommended for large bags
- **Booking:**
  - 1 month in advance recommended for peak travel times
  - Book online via JR East/West websites or at ticket offices
  - Seat selection available when booking
  - Window seats (A/E) and aisle seats (C/D) in standard cars
- **Onboard amenities:**
  - Power outlets at most seats (check when booking)
  - Vending machines and food carts
  - Clean restrooms with Western and Japanese toilets
  - Free Wi-Fi on most trains
  - Eating and drinking allowed (unlike local trains)

### Tokyo Metro (Subway)

**What it is:** Tokyo's main subway system with 9 color-coded lines covering central Tokyo.

**Most Useful Lines for Tourists:**

- **Ginza Line** (orange) - Shibuya, Ginza, Asakusa
- **Hibiya Line** (gray) - Roppongi, Ginza, Tsukiji
- **Marunouchi Line** (red) - Tokyo Station, Ginza, Shinjuku
- **Hanzomon Line** (purple) - Shibuya to Skytree area
- **Fukutoshin Line** (brown) - Shibuya to Ikebukuro

**Tips:**

- Separate from JR system (different tickets/passes)
- Very clean, safe, and punctual
- Can get crowded during rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM)

### Toei Subway

**What it is:** Tokyo's secondary subway system operated by Tokyo Metropolitan Government (4 lines).

**Key Lines:**

- **Toei Asakusa Line** - Connects to Haneda Airport
- **Toei Oedo Line** - Large loop including Roppongi and Shinjuku

**Note:** Separate from Tokyo Metro (different tickets), but IC cards work on both.

### Private Railways

**What they are:** Independent railway companies operating specific routes.

**Key Private Lines:**

- **Odakyu Line** - Shinjuku to Hakone (Romancecar limited express)
- **Keikyu Line** - Shinagawa to Haneda Airport
- **Keihan Line** - Kyoto to Osaka, stops at Fushimi Inari
- **Kintetsu Line** - Kyoto/Osaka to Nara (faster than JR)
- **Hankyu Line** - Osaka to Kyoto and Kobe

## IC Cards (Rechargeable Smart Cards)

**What they are:** Prepaid cards that work on trains, buses, and even convenience stores.

**Main Cards:**

- **Suica** (JR East) - Penguin logo
- **Pasmo** (Tokyo Metro/Private railways) - Robot logo
- **ICOCA** (JR West, Kansai region) - Platypus logo

**How they work:**

1. Purchase at any major station ticket machine (¥500 deposit + initial charge)
2. Tap card on reader when entering and exiting stations
3. Recharge at any ticket machine or convenience store
4. Works nationwide on almost all trains, buses, and even taxis
5. Can be used at convenience stores, vending machines, and many shops

**Benefits:**

- No need to buy individual tickets
- Faster than buying tickets each time
- Works across different train companies
- Automatic fare calculation
- Can be shared among family (one person taps multiple times)

**Recommended amount:** Load ¥2,000-3,000 per person to start, recharge as needed

**Where to buy:**

- Haneda/Narita Airport ticket machines
- Any JR station ticket machine
- Major subway stations

## Bus Systems

### City Buses (Tokyo)

**What they are:** Local buses covering areas not served by trains.

**How to use:**

- **Flat fare system** in central Tokyo (usually ¥210-230 per ride)
- **Boarding process:**
  - Board through front door (some buses use back door)
  - Take a numbered ticket if fare varies by distance
  - Exit through middle door
  - Pay when exiting (cash or IC card)
- **IC card is easiest:**
  - Tap when boarding and exiting
  - Automatic fare calculation
  - No need for exact change
- **Cash payment:**
  - Exact change required (¥210-230)
  - Change machines available on bus (can break ¥1,000 notes)
  - Insert ticket and payment in fare box when exiting
- **Route information:**
  - Route numbers and destinations displayed in English at major stops
  - Digital displays show next stop in English
  - Announcements in Japanese and English at major stops
  - Bus stop poles show route maps

**When to use:**

- Roppongi to Tokyo Tower (Toei Bus #01)
- Shibuya to Roppongi (Toei Bus #01)
- Areas between train stations
- Late night when trains stop running (midnight-5 AM)
- Rainy days to avoid walking between stations

**Key Tokyo Bus Routes for Tourists:**

- **Toei Bus #01** - Shibuya to Roppongi to Tokyo Tower
- **Toei Bus #都01** - Tokyo Station to Roppongi
- **Toei Bus #東98** - Tokyo Station to Tsukiji
- **Toei Bus #S-01** - Shinjuku to Shibuya (late night service)

**Tips:**

- Google Maps shows bus routes and real-time schedules
- Buses can be slower than trains due to traffic (allow extra time)
- Less English signage than trains, but improving
- Bus drivers generally don't speak English
- Priority seating for elderly, pregnant, and disabled passengers
- No eating or drinking on buses
- Press stop button before your destination

### Kyoto City Buses

**What they are:** Extensive bus network covering Kyoto's temples and attractions. Essential for reaching many temples not served by trains.

**How to use:**

- **Flat fare zone:** ¥230 per ride within city center (most tourist areas)
- **Boarding process:**
  - Board through **back door**
  - Take numbered ticket from machine if traveling outside flat fare zone
  - Exit through **front door**
  - Pay when exiting (cash or IC card)
  - Show bus pass to driver when exiting
- **IC card:**
  - Tap when boarding and exiting
  - Automatic fare calculation
  - Works on all Kyoto buses
- **Cash payment:**
  - Exact change required (¥230)
  - Change machine near driver (can break ¥1,000 notes)
  - Insert ticket (if taken) and payment in fare box

**Key Routes for Tourists:**

- **#100** - Kyoto Station → Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) via Gion
  - Stops: Kyoto Station → Gion → Ginkaku-ji → Kinkaku-ji
  - Tourist-friendly with English announcements
  - Can be very crowded
- **#101, #102** - Kyoto Station to Kinkaku-ji (alternative routes)
- **#204** - Circular route (clockwise) connecting major temples
  - Stops: Kyoto Station → Kiyomizu-dera → Gion → Ginkaku-ji → Kitaoji
  - Most useful tourist route
- **#205** - Circular route (counter-clockwise)
  - Opposite direction of #204
- **#206** - Kyoto Station to Kiyomizu-dera
  - Direct route to popular temple
  - Very crowded during peak season
- **#5** - Kyoto Station to Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion)
  - Stops at Heian Shrine
- **Raku Bus (100, 101, 102)** - Special tourist buses
  - Designed for tourists with English announcements
  - Slightly more expensive but less crowded
  - Distinctive green color

**Kyoto Bus Pass:**

- **1-Day Pass:** ¥700 (worthwhile if taking 3+ bus rides)
- **2-Day Pass:** ¥1,400
- **Where to buy:**
  - Kyoto Station bus terminal ticket office
  - On the bus from driver (exact change helpful)
  - Convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart)
  - Hotels and tourist information centers
- **How to use:**
  - Scratch off date on first use
  - Show pass to driver when exiting
  - Unlimited rides on city buses within flat fare zone
  - Does NOT cover subway or trains
- **Coverage:** All Kyoto City Bus routes in flat fare zone (most tourist areas)

**Kyoto Bus Terminal (Kyoto Station):**

- **Location:** North side of Kyoto Station
- **Layout:** Multiple platforms (A1-D3) organized by route
- **Information desk:** English-speaking staff available
- **Digital displays:** Show next bus arrival times in English
- **Route maps:** Available in English at information desk
- **Facilities:** Restrooms, vending machines, seating area

**Tips:**

- **Crowding:** Buses can be extremely crowded, especially:
  - Cherry blossom season (late March-early April)
  - Fall foliage season (November)
  - Weekends and holidays
  - Routes to Kinkaku-ji and Kiyomizu-dera
- **Alternative:** Consider trains when available (faster and less crowded)
  - Subway to Karasuma-Oike, then bus
  - JR Sagano Line to Arashiyama (instead of bus)
  - Keihan Line to Fushimi Inari (instead of bus)
- **Timing:**
  - Buses run 6:00 AM - 11:00 PM
  - Less frequent service after 8:00 PM
  - Allow extra time during peak hours
- **Navigation:**
  - Bus stops have route maps in English
  - Google Maps shows real-time bus locations
  - Kyoto Station bus terminal has English information desk
  - Bus stop announcements in Japanese and English
- **Etiquette:**
  - Queue in line at bus stops
  - Let passengers exit before boarding
  - Move to back of bus to make room
  - No eating or drinking on buses
  - Keep voices low
  - Priority seating for elderly, pregnant, disabled

### Highway Buses (Long Distance)

**What they are:** Comfortable coaches for intercity travel, cheaper alternative to trains.

**Common Routes:**

- Tokyo to Kyoto/Osaka (overnight buses available)
- Tokyo to Mount Fuji/Hakone
- Osaka to Hiroshima

**When to use:**

- Budget travel (much cheaper than Shinkansen)
- Overnight travel to save on accommodation
- Scenic routes (e.g., to Mount Fuji)

**Booking:**

- [Willer Express](https://willerexpress.com/en/) - English booking site
- [Japan Bus Online](https://japanbusonline.com/en) - Multiple bus companies
- Book in advance for popular routes

**Note:** For this itinerary, trains are recommended for speed and convenience with family.

### Airport Buses (Limousine Buses)

**What they are:** Direct buses from airports to major hotels and districts.

**Haneda Airport:**

- Direct buses to Shibuya, Shinjuku, Tokyo Station, Ginza
- ¥1,000-1,300 per person
- 45-90 minutes depending on destination and traffic
- Luggage storage underneath
- Comfortable but slower than trains during rush hour

**When to use:**

- Direct route to your hotel
- Heavy luggage
- Traveling with young children
- Late arrival when trains are less frequent

## Special Transportation Passes

### Hakone Free Pass

**What it covers:** All transportation in Hakone area for 2 or 3 days

- Odakyu trains from Shinjuku to Hakone
- Hakone Tozan Train, Cable Car, Ropeway
- Lake Ashi cruise
- Local buses in Hakone
- Discounts at museums and attractions

**Cost:** ¥6,100 (2-day) or ¥6,500 (3-day) from Shinjuku
**Where to buy:** Odakyu Shinjuku Station or online
**Worth it?** Yes, if doing the full Hakone loop

### JR Pass (Japan Rail Pass)

**What it covers:** Unlimited JR trains nationwide including most Shinkansen (except Nozomi/Mizuho)

**Cost:** 7-day pass ~¥50,000 for adults
**Worth it for this itinerary?**

- **Probably NOT** - You only take 2 Shinkansen trips (Hakone-Kyoto, Osaka-Tokyo)
- Individual Shinkansen tickets: ~¥26,000 total
- JR Pass: ~¥50,000 per adult
- **Better option:** Buy individual Shinkansen tickets + use IC card for local transport

**When JR Pass IS worth it:**

- Multiple long-distance Shinkansen trips
- Trips to more distant cities (Hiroshima, Hokkaido, etc.)
- Extensive JR train use in Tokyo/Kyoto

## Navigation Apps

### Google Maps

**Best for:** Route planning, real-time directions, English interface

- Shows all train and bus options
- Real-time delays and platform information
- Walking directions between stations
- Works offline if you download maps in advance
- **Tip:** Set departure time to see schedule options

### Hyperdia

**Best for:** Detailed train schedules and route options

- Website: [hyperdia.com](https://www.hyperdia.com/en/)
- More detailed than Google Maps for trains
- Shows exact train types and car numbers
- Can filter by JR Pass coverage
- English interface available

### Japan Transit Planner (Jorudan)

**Best for:** Alternative to Hyperdia with similar features

- App available for iOS and Android
- Shows fare breakdowns
- Platform information
- English support

## General Tips

**Train Etiquette:**

- Queue in lines marked on platform
- Let passengers exit before boarding
- Priority seating for elderly, pregnant, disabled
- Keep voices low, phone on silent
- No eating on local trains (OK on Shinkansen)
- No phone calls on trains

**Rush Hour Avoidance:**

- Morning: 7:00-9:00 AM (worst 8:00-8:30 AM)
- Evening: 5:00-7:00 PM (worst 6:00-6:30 PM)
- Women-only cars available during rush hour (usually first or last car)

**Station Navigation:**

- Major stations have English signs
- Follow color-coded lines
- Platform numbers clearly marked
- Station staff usually at ticket gates (some speak English)
- Free Wi-Fi at most major stations

**Luggage on Trains:**

- Overhead racks for small/medium bags
- Space behind last row seats for large luggage
- Coin lockers at major stations (¥300-700 per day)
- **Luggage forwarding (takkyubin)** recommended for hotel-to-hotel transfers
  - Send bags 1-2 days ahead
  - ¥2,000-2,500 per bag
  - Arrange at hotel front desk or convenience stores

**Money-Saving Tips:**

- IC card is most convenient and cost-effective for local transport
- Buy Shinkansen tickets in advance for reserved seats
- Consider bus passes in Kyoto if taking multiple bus rides
- Walk when possible - many attractions are close together
- Avoid taxis except when necessary (expensive, ~¥700 base fare)

**Emergency Phrases:**

- "Sumimasen" (Excuse me) - to get attention
- "Eigo ga hanasemasu ka?" (Do you speak English?)
- "Kono eki wa doko desu ka?" (Where is this station?)
- Most station staff can help even with limited English
